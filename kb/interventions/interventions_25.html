Embrace heat tourism

Heat tourism refers to travel to destinations known for their warm or hot climates, often during cooler months in tourists' home countries. Many regions are embracing heat tourism as a way to boost their economy, extend tourism seasons, and promote wellness tourism.

Example of Heat Tourism Destinations:

  • Canary Islands, Spain – A popular year-round sun destination.
  • Algarve, Portugal – Known for its mild winters and coastal retreats.
  • Crete, Greece – Attracts off-season visitors with cultural and wellness tourism.
  • Southern Italy (Sicily, Puglia) – Promotes winter escapes with food, culture, and warm weather.
  • Malta – A Mediterranean island with history, nature, and year-round sunshine.

Benefits

  • Boosts Local Economies & Jobs – Increases revenue for hotels, restaurants, and tour operators while creating employment opportunities.
  • Extends the Tourism Season – Encourages year-round travel, reducing dependence on peak summer months.
  • Promotes Health & Wellness – Warmer climates support relaxation, outdoor activities, and health benefits like arthritis relief.
  • Encourages Sustainable & Cultural Tourism – Supports eco-friendly travel, slow tourism, and engagement with local traditions.
  • Aids Climate Adaptation & Diversification – Helps destinations reposition as winter sun getaways and expand tourism offerings beyond summer.

Criticism or Limitations

  • Extreme Heat Risks – Destinations must manage the risks of rising temperatures and ensure visitor safety.
  • Water & Resource Management – Increased tourism in hot areas can put pressure on water supplies.
  • Sustainable Practices Needed – Growth in heat tourism should be balanced with eco-friendly initiatives.
  • Climate Change Impacts – Some regions may become too hot for tourism if adaptation strategies are not in place.
