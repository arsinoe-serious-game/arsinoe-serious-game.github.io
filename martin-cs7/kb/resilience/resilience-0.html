Economic Resilience Challenge

Economic resilience challenges occur when there is a loss of confidence in the economy. This can be seen as a reduction in investment, leading company closures, job losses, and a general reduction in consumer spending . Severity is a product of impact and duration; extreme events will be deeper and longer than mild ones.

Minor Resilience Challenge

A large local employer closes down. All of a sudden, many people are out of work and companies that rely on the closed company have a reduction in their turnover.

Examples

Fudge Factory Closes
Vauxhall Closes Luton van Factory
Electronics Factory closes

Average Resilience Challenge

Bad weather impacts the summer holiday season. This results in many local businesses being impacted by a downturn in their trade.

Some businesses will be able to 'weather the storm', but for smaller businesses that rely on the summer season, a washout will mean the end of their business.

Examples

Cornwall's tourism boss has urged people to "have faith" in the county after a wet Easter.
Tourism at 'lowest point for 10 years'
Builders out of pocket from continual bad weather

Major Resilience Challenge

A global credit crunch, economic decisions in a country far, far, away impact the town's economy by removing money from the economy. This results in some international companies closing their operations in the town, increased costs for goods and services and a reluctance by people to spend their limited cash.

Examples

Credit crunch takes time to bite
People turn from banks to pawnbrokers
Credit Crunch impact on Devon
