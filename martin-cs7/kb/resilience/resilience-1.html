Biodiversity Resilience Challenge

Biodiversity resilience challenges occur when the fauna and or flora of an environment is threatened by external sources. Typically, this may occur as a side effect of industrial pollution, or the introduction of non indigenous flora and fauna. 

Minor Resilience Challenge

A local diary farm has a storage failure, releasing milk into the local river, killing all the fish. Whilst the leak is repaired quickly and the dead fish removed, it takes considerable time for the river to return to pristine condition.

Examples

10,000 fish dead after toxic pollution spill in Devon river
Brixham: More than 50 people in Devon ill from contaminated water
One in 10 neighbourhoods 'unsafe for wildlife'

Average Resilience Challenge

An oil tanker runs aground up the coast, spilling a million barrels of oil, wiping out much of the bird life and destroying the local beach tourism industry for a couple of seasons. Residents and businesses are starting to get nervous about the future.

Examples

Torrey Canyon oil spill: The day the sea turned black
27 years on: A look back at the Sea Empress oil spill
Atlantic Empress/Aegean Captain

Major Resilience Challenge

A local farm uses a pesticide that destroys the local bee population. Without bees, local crops and plants are not pollinated, resulting in a commercial and environmental collapse. Some residents and businesses are now looking to move away to areas that have a better quality of life.

Examples

Dartmoor’s disappearing areas could spell climate disaster
Cornwall’s nature is in decline as G7 leaders meet to discuss global issues
