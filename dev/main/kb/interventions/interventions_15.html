An Out-of-Town Leisure Resort is a large recreational facility located outside urban areas, often on the outskirts of cities or in rural settings. These resorts provide a variety of sports, fitness, and entertainment facilities designed to attract visitors from a wide area.

Key Features of Miyawaki Forests:

  • Location: Situated outside city centres, often near major roads or highways for easy access.
  • Facilities: May include gyms, swimming pools, sports courts, golf courses, cinemas, bowling alleys, and outdoor adventure spaces.
  • Parking & Accessibility: Usually offers large parking areas and good transport links.
  • Target Audience: Appeals to families, fitness enthusiasts, and tourists looking for recreational activities.
  • Lower Land Costs: Cheaper land allows for larger, more spacious facilities compared to city-based centres.

Benefits

  • More space for larger and diverse facilities.
  • Less congestion compared to city-based centres.
  • Lower land and operational costs.
  • Easier parking and access for visitors.

Criticism or Limitations

  • Requires private transport, limiting accessibility for some people.
  • Can contribute to urban sprawl and environmental concerns.
  • Less convenient for those living in city centres.
  • Can impact local businesses if it draws customers away from town centres.
